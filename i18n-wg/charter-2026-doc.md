This **Disposition of Comments** identifies the feedback received during the review of the 2026 [W3C Internationalization Working Group Charter](https://w3c.github.io/i18n-activity/i18n-wg/charter.html) and describes how those issues were resolved.

# Disposition of Comments: Internationalization Working Group Charter

| # | Submitter | Topic | Comment | Resolution |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| 1 | Dominique Hazael-Massieux | Accuracy | Several sections mention "the past 3 years," but these appear unchanged from the previous charter version despite the timeline shifting. | **Accepted.** The charter text has been updated to accurately reflect the elapsed time since the beginning of the current period. |
| 2 | Dominique Hazael-Massieux | Template Alignment | Sections 5 through 8 require closer alignment with the latest official W3C charter template. | **Accepted.** Updates were made to the indicated sections to ensure consistency with the current W3C chartering process and templates. |
| 3 | Dominique Hazael-Massieux | Scope & Normative Work | The Scope section should explicitly state that the WG develops normative specifications, as these are already included in the Deliverables. | **Accepted.** The Scope section has been revised to clarify the Working Group's mandate to produce both normative and non-normative specifications. |
